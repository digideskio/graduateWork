Who’s really involved?

Epidemiologists

  • Determine existence of an outbreak
  • Determine a case definition
  • Conduct interviews with cases, family members, contacts and controls
  • Generate a hypothesis
  • Design an appropriate study (if necessary)
  • Review, analyze, and interpret interview, analytic study, and laboratory data
http://cphp.sph.unc.edu/trainingpackages/E/Session2/Part1/Session2Part1handouts.pdf

Epidemiologists

Answer what is the causative agent of the outbreak and how can the epidemic be contained.


Work with first responders, environmental health specialists, and public health laboratory staff to effectively address and contain the outbreak.

Environmental Health Specialists


Through collection and testing of food, water, and other environmental variables of interest, determines:


“WHY was this item responsible for infection?”

Environmental Health Specialists

In an outbreak, provides a systematic, detailed, science-based evaluation of environmental factors that contributed to the transmission of a particular disease.


(I.e., how did the causative agent, host factors, and environmental conditions interact to result in the problem?)

Public Health Laboratories

  • Disease prevention, control, and surveillance
  • Integrated data management
  • Reference and specialized testing
  • Environmental health and protection
  • Food safety
  • Policy development
  • Laboratory improvement and regulation
  • Public health research
  • Training and education
  • Emergency preparedness and response
  • Partnerships and communication

Public Health Laboratories


“The ongoing systematic collection, analysis, and interpretation of health data, essential to the planning, implementation, and evaluation of public health practice, closely integrated with the timely dissemination to those who need to know.”
